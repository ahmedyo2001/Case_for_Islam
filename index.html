Step 1 · God's existence
Does God exist?
"Before anything else, we must ask: is there a creator at all?"
The cosmological argument+
Everything that begins to exist has a cause. The universe began to exist — confirmed by modern cosmology and the Big Bang. Therefore the universe has a cause that itself did not begin: an uncaused, eternal, immensely powerful being.
"The universe had a beginning, and therefore has a cause beyond itself."
The fine-tuning argument+
The fundamental constants of the universe — gravity, the strong nuclear force, the cosmological constant — are calibrated to extraordinary precision. A tiny deviation in any of them and no stars, no planets, no life could exist. The probability of this arising by chance is astronomically small. Design is the most rational explanation.
The moral argument+
Objective moral values exist — some things are wrong regardless of culture or opinion. But objective morality requires an objective moral standard beyond human invention. That standard cannot be grounded in matter or evolution alone. It requires a transcendent moral lawgiver: God.

Step 2 · God's oneness
Is there only one God?
"If a creator exists, is monotheism the only coherent position?"
The argument from omnipotence+
True omnipotence cannot be shared. Two omnipotent beings would inevitably conflict — and whichever one gets overruled is not omnipotent. Therefore only one omnipotent being can exist.
The argument from simplicity+
A single uncaused being is the most parsimonious explanation for the universe. Postulating multiple gods raises further unanswered questions: who created them? How do they coordinate? Occam's razor favours one.
The unified order argument+
The universe operates under a single, consistent set of physical laws across billions of light years. This unified coherence points to a single will behind creation — not a committee of competing gods with conflicting visions.

Step 3 · Revelation
Would God send guidance?
"If God exists and is one, would He communicate with His creation?"
The argument from purpose+
A God who creates humanity with consciousness, moral awareness, and the capacity to ask "why am I here?" — and then provides no answer — is inconsistent with perfect wisdom and care. If God is all-knowing and all-caring, revelation follows necessarily.
The argument from human need+
Every human civilisation across history has sought guidance on how to live — evidence of a deep, universal need. A just God who implanted this need would not leave it unaddressed. The very hunger for meaning points to the existence of something that satisfies it.

Step 4 · Authenticity of the Quran
Is the Quran from God?
"Which text — if any — is genuinely divine?"
The literary miracle+
The Quran challenged the finest poets and linguists of Arabia to produce even one chapter comparable to it. Fourteen centuries later, that challenge stands unmet. Even Islam's fiercest early opponents acknowledged its unparalleled linguistic power.
"This is not the speech of a human being." — Walid ibn al-Mughira, one of Islam's earliest opponents
Perfect preservation+
The Quran is the only ancient scripture with an unbroken chain of memorisation spanning 1400 years. Millions today recite the exact same text. No other religious scripture can claim this degree of verified, documented preservation.
Scientific foreknowledge+
The Quran describes embryological development, the expansion of the universe, and oceanographic phenomena — details that were unknown to 7th century Arabia and only confirmed by modern science centuries later.

Step 5 · Prophethood
Was Muhammad ﷺ a true prophet?
"Was he genuinely sent by God — or a fraud, a madman, or simply mistaken?"
The character argument+
Before prophethood, Muhammad ﷺ was known unanimously as Al-Amin — the trustworthy. His enemies, who rejected his message fiercely, never accused him of lying in his personal life. A man of such established character does not fabricate the most consequential claim in history.
No worldly motive+
Muhammad ﷺ gained nothing materially for the first 13 years of his mission — only persecution, boycott, and the death of his closest loved ones. He was offered wealth, kingship, and fame by the Quraysh to stop. He refused every offer. Frauds do not pay this price.
"By God, if they placed the sun in my right hand and the moon in my left, I would not abandon this matter."
The civilisational transformation+
An illiterate man in 7th century Arabia produced within 23 years a complete civilisational framework — legal, spiritual, and scientific — that transformed a fragmented tribal society into a global civilisation spanning continents. No naturalistic explanation is adequate for this transformation.
